Is the Middleman Tax Killing Your GovCon Margins? Here's the Truth

Published: March 14, 2026

The federal market is not a meritocracy. It is a game of positioning. If you are currently operating as a subcontractor, you are paying a tax. We call it the Middleman Tax.

What Is the Middleman Tax?

The Middleman Tax is the financial and strategic delta between being the lead and being the help. When you operate as a sub, the prime takes 15–30% off the top. You have zero direct relationship with the CO and your past performance is credited to their resume.

The Sub-to-Prime Chasm

Most firms treat subcontracting as a "stepping stone." This is a dangerous fallacy. Subcontracting is often quicksand. The more you sub, the more you build the prime’s past performance, remaining invisible to the agencies you want to serve.

Market Telemetry: The $500,000 Reality Check

On a standard $500,000 service contract, a prime markup can cost you $100,000. Over five years, that is $450,000 in "tax" paid to a prime contractor. That is the cost of not having a Roadmap.

Actionable Intelligence: Audit Your Margin Today

  1. Log in to SAM.gov and navigate to the "Data Bank."
  2. Run an Ad Hoc Report on your own UEI.
  3. Calculate the "Margin Leak": Total revenue as sub multiplied by 0.20.

Bottom Line

The Middleman Tax is a choice. Build your path to prime status using a data-driven Roadmap.
