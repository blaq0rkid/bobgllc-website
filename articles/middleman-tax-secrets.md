If an evaluator suspects you are a "shell," your proposal is dead on arrival. They aren't just looking for the lowest price; they are looking for the lowest risk. A Prime who doesn't understand the work is a massive risk.

## Tier 4: Proposal Management — Turn “Markup” into “Operational Infrastructure”

When you get to **Tier 4: Proposal Management**, the defense of your markup becomes a technical writing exercise. You cannot simply state your G&A and Profit. You must justify them through the lens of "Project Management Excellence."

A Prime Contractor Strategy that wins involves detailing your "Program Management Office" (PMO) functions. This includes:

- Quality Assurance/Quality Control (QA/QC) protocols.
- Financial transparency and reporting.
- Subcontractor management and vetting.
- Compliance with FAR/DFARS requirements.

By breaking down these costs, you transform "markup" into "operational infrastructure." Evaluators at the VA or Navy are far more likely to accept a 15% markup if they see it funds a dedicated Quality Manager and a 24/7 help desk.

## Actionable Intelligence (SAM.gov): Build a Saved Search for Competitor Awards

Stop guessing what the market will bear. Use SAM.gov to benchmark real awards. Your fastest win is a saved search that you can review weekly.

1. Log into [SAM.gov](https://sam.gov).
2. Go to **Contract Opportunities** and select **Award Notices** (filter).
3. Set **NAICS** to your primary code and set **Award Date** to the last 12 months.
4. Set **Place of Performance** (optional) and add keywords tied to your scope (ex: "help desk", "cyber", "EHR").
5. In **Awardee Name**, test a large competitor and a small competitor (run twice).
6. Click **Save Search**. Name it: *Navy/VA/DHA Awards — NAICS ###### — Markup Benchmark*.
